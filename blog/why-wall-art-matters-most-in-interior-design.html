Why Wall Art Matters Most In Interior Design


Integrate wall art into your decor 101
Do you consider
yourself
adventurous?

ART COMPASS Issue 28


Art, It is what gets dealt with last, long after the last coat of paint has dried on the walls and all the furniture has been arranged. Moreover, sometimes, it is not dealt with at all. However, we are here to assert that by relegating wall art to the sidelines, you are missing out on a significant design opportunity. When selected thoughtfully, wall art interior design can provide for the whole room. Nothing adds personality and color quite like a gallery wall. Display a collection of art, photographs, or add wall hangings and other ephemera. Opt for simple, cohesive frames or bring in an array of ornate variations to mix things up!

We believe fine wall art matters most in interior design. However, if you're a bit nervous about providing fine wall art as an essential part of your plan, do not worry. You can use this blog post as a guide on how to correctly choose pieces which will balance your current space and you'll have a harmonious interior.

Choosing a color palette may be among the most daunting aspects of designing your insides. The number of varying colors of paint which can be found at your local home improvement store can seem infinite. It can be challenging to decide the colors that perfectly match your vision to your interior space. The best piece of advice is to leave the paint chips and focus on searching for wall art instead. As soon as you find an artwork you love, you can use that bit as the inspiration for your interiors' ultimate color palette.

Step one is to choose two or three colors from wall art that you want to integrate into your decor. Pick the dominant color, in addition to a few additional colors that you want to pull out as accents. Then, search for all those colors in the things which you use to decorate your space. If you need more assistance, use ColorSnap, which will enable you to match to corresponding shades of paint. Let artwork bring out your accent colors. It Produces a Focal Point.

A crucial principle of interior design is that every room needs a focal point. The design element will immediately draw the eye to the place and give the viewer a feeling of what to expect. A significant part of wall art could easily meet this position. Imagine your favorite art print hanging in your living room or suite.

A creative gallery wall could easily upgrade a more conventional dining room, or a few prints could build a cozy feel to a seating area. When selecting a piece of wall art as a focus, the consideration is the size. An oversize painting or photograph will command attention and set the tone in a small space. Try a black-and-white photo in a minimalist space or add color with a vibrant abstract piece.

An artwork that's too small will get dwarfed by the surrounding furniture. If it is too large will look as though it's spilling over. Take measurements of the walls, so you know for sure how much space you've got at your disposal. Use artwork to create a focus for the remainder of the area.

Bear in mind that not all fine art is made equal. Though some pieces might be two-dimensional paintings or something similar, you should try to find artwork in various mediums to help bring a varying awareness of feel into space. In addition to art prints, canvas prints, and paintings, you need to consider sculptures or shadow boxes which will add some depth to the area. For more avant-garde design, you can also consider doing a tiny mixed media installation that includes screens and electronic artwork. These additional pieces can help add essential visual weight to your insides, which help determine the tone and the feel of the room.

Rough textures are more inclined to make a room feel cozy and grounded. Smooth textures bring a sleeker tone into the room. Do not just stick to paintings. Think about some of the less-than-focused interiors that you have seen. Maybe a college apartment or a first adult space after completing school. The odds are that these spaces felt a bit rough around the edges and a bit imperfect and had mostly white walls.

Wall art is that completing element which could help pull a room together and make it feel complete. It's that little extra touch that could take your space from just looking practical to appearing like it ought to grace the pages of an interior design magazine. The important thing is to pick a piece of art or a different wall hanging which fits in with the decorating design that you have already chosen for space. Then, it's all about picking decor that you love and will be happy seeing hang on your wall for several years to come.

Art helps separate a completed room from a random space. Wall art doesn't need to be the final piece of the puzzle when you are decorating a new area. In our heads, wall art is the most significant when it comes to interior design. This is because when it is used correctly, your wall hangings can provide a superb framework around which you ought to have the ability to plan the remainder of the room.

Take the above post as a manual for how to correctly select and integrate wall art into your space, and you will wind up getting a layout that looks like it was put together by a pro.

How much importance do you place on fine art when decorating a space? At what point in the design process do you typically pick your artwork? Tell us in the comments below.

About author Robert McIntosh was born 1965 in Amsterdam, North Holland. He studied at Royal College of Art in South Kensington. Educated in England and the United States. Lives in Amsterdam. Has also lived in Russia. He works mainly with oil paintings and sculptures and is interested only in authentic and genuine art. Modern Art. Contemporary Art. He writes extensively on abstract art.
